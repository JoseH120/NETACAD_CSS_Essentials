The collapsing parent problem

After applying the float property to elements, these elements are taken out of the normal document flow. This means that they don't affect the layout of subsequent elements the usual way.

Clearing floats with the clearfix technique

After using floated columns, It's important to clear the floats to maintain the normal flow for subsequent elements.

in the previous section you learned about the clear property which we used directly on a specific element to prevent it from being adjacent to floated elements on either or both sides. This time, we'll use the clearfix, which will be applied to a parent container of floated elements. We will use it to clear the floats of its children so that the parent container doesn't collapse and correctly contains the floated elements.

Clearfix works by adding a pseudo-element, such as ::after, to the parent container. This pseudo-element is styled in a way that it clears the float, thus making the container recognize the heightof its floated children.

In our code:

  • We use the ::after pseudo-element, which is a special type of selector in CSS, that allows us to insert content after the content of an element. In the case of clearfix, we use it to insert an invisible "element" at the end of the container.
  • the content:""; rule, which is essential for the pseudo-element to work, generates content, but we keep it empty
  • the display:table; rule changes the display type, which helps in the clearing process.
  • the clear:both; rule clears the float from both the left and right sides. This action forces the container to expand and encompass the floated elements, thus solving the collapsing issue.

Why and when to use clearfix

  • to ensure that a parent container fully wraps around its floated children
  • to maintain the intented layout, particularly when subsequent content follows the floated elements.
  • as a cleaner solution compared to adding extra non-semantic markup (like empty div elements) to clear floats

Clear vs Clearfix

In general

  • clear is about managing the layout flow of individual elements in relation to floated items.
  • clearfix is a broader technique to ensure that a container property its floated children, thus maintaining the integrity of the overall layout

In other words, use clear to fine-tune specific elements, and use the clearfix technique to address the layout behavior of an entire container

The overflow method

Before we move on, we want to show you one more method to clear floats besides the traditional clear and the clearfix technique - the overflow property.

You can use the overflow property on the parent container. This is a simpler method but can have unintended effects if your content is meant to extend outside the container.
