How to use flexbox

Step 1

The first step in using Flexbox is to define a Flex container. This container will hold the flex items(your content) and control their layout.

Step 2

Once you have a Flex container, the second step is to style and align the Flex items within it.

Flexbox provides properties like justify-content and align-items to control the layout along the main and cross axes.

As we mentioned earlier, the justify-content property manages the spacing and alignment of items along the main axis (horizontally), while the align-items property does the same along the cross axis (vertically). These properties are key to structuring your flex items within the container.

Step 3

finally, you can customize individual flex items according to your design needs - apply CSS styles to the Flex items to control their size, color, margins, paddings, and so on.

Flexbox offers several properties that give you control over spacing (margin, paddin), alignment (aling-self, aling-items), and the order (order) of flex items.

You can also use flex-grow, flex-shrink, and flex-basis to control how items grow or shrink in relation to each other and the available space.
