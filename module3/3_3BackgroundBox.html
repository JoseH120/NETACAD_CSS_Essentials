Controlling background images

let's explore hhow to exert more precise control over the display of background images and their interactions with other box models properties, such as padding and borders, using featuers like background-size and background-clip

you probably remember that the background-size CSS property specifies the size of the element's background image. To be more specific, it determines how the image should be scaled and stretched within the element.

The background-clip property, on the other hand, defines how far the background(color or image) extends within an element. This property becomes particularly useful when you have border and padding applied to your element. The values for background-clip are:

  • border-box - the background extends to the outside edge of the border (default value). If the element has a border, the background will be underneath it.
  • padding-box - the background extends to the outside edge of the padding. No background is drawn beneath the border.
  • content-box - the background is painted within (and clipped to) the content box only.
